The gap nobody is naming

You already know something is off. You are using AI to draft intake forms, summarize session notes, build content, research protocols. Some of that is completely fine. Some of it carries real liability exposure. And right now, you have no system for telling the difference.

That is the governance gap. Not a skills problem. Not a tech problem. A classification problem. Which of your AI workflows are safe to run as-is? Which ones need guardrails? Which ones should not exist in your practice at all?

Without answers to those questions, every AI tool you touch is an unmanaged variable in a clinical environment.

The question is not "should I use AI?" You are already using it. The question is whether you have a framework that protects your clients, your scope, and your professional judgment.

The Red / Yellow / Green Zone Model

Every AI workflow in your practice falls into one of three zones. Knowing which zone you are operating in changes what you do next.

Green Zone
Cleared for use. Minimal clinical risk. AI is working as an efficiency tool, not touching the decision layer. Administrative workflows, scheduling, content drafting, research organization. These are fine. Run them.
Yellow Zone
Proceed with guardrails. Real value, real risk if unmanaged. Session summaries, protocol research, client communication drafts. The AI output is useful, but it needs a Human Authority Filter before it reaches your client or influences your clinical decisions.
Red Zone
Pause and redesign. Liability exposure, scope-of-practice risk, or clinical quality concerns. If AI is making recommendations you are passing to clients without independent verification, that workflow needs to stop until governance is in place.

This is the framework. The Audit applies it to your specific practice, your specific tools, your specific client population. The Assessment gives you a written classification of where you stand right now.
